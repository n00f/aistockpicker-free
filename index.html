Why

    Indicator Guide

    • Price: Latest closing price for the stock.
    • 5D Change: Percent change over the last 5 trading days. Positive means short-term momentum is up.
    • 20D Change: Percent change over the last 20 trading days. Positive is usually a healthier trend signal.
    • SMA 20: 20-day simple moving average. If price is above this, short-term trend is generally stronger.
    • SMA 50: 50-day simple moving average. Used to judge medium-term trend direction.
    • RSI 14: Momentum oscillator from 0 to 100. Very high can mean overbought; very low can mean oversold.
    • Volatility (20D ann.): How much price tends to swing, annualized from the last 20 days. Lower is usually less risky.
    • Volume vs 20D avg: Today’s volume divided by 20-day average volume. Above 1.0x means heavier-than-usual trading activity.
    • Score (x/5): Rule-based strength score from five checks: price above SMA20, SMA20 above SMA50, positive 20D return, RSI in a constructive range, and moderate volatility.
    • Verdict: 4-5 = GOOD PICK, 3 = WATCHLIST, 0-2 = HIGH RISK.
